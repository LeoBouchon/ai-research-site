AI for Medical Imaging

Deep learning models for multi-disease detection from clinical radiographs — built at the Stanford AIMI Center and beyond.

Research Mission

My research focuses on applying convolutional neural networks and transfer learning to medical image classification, with a particular emphasis on chest radiography. The clinical challenge is significant: many thoracic conditions share overlapping visual and symptomatic features, making accurate multi-label classification both medically valuable and technically demanding.

Each project below follows a rigorous pipeline — from dataset preprocessing and model architecture selection through training, evaluation, and clinical interpretation of results. I document not just what worked, but what didn't, and why.

Chest X-Ray · CheXpert Plus · DenseNet121

Multi-Disease Lung Pathology Detection

A DenseNet121 classifier trained on 223,462 chest radiographs to simultaneously detect 5 thoracic pathologies: Atelectasis, Cardiomegaly, Consolidation, Edema, and Pleural Effusion. Achieved a mean validation AUROC of 0.9011 — a 13.3% improvement over the DenseNet121 baseline.

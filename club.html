What Is This Club?

The AI Club at Monta Vista is a student-run space to go deep on how artificial intelligence actually works — not just conceptually, but technically. Sessions are designed by a working AI researcher (me) and draw directly from real projects built at Stanford.

No prior experience is required. Whether you've never written a line of Python or you've already trained your first model, there's something here for you. We move at a pace that challenges beginners and adds depth for those already in the field.

Planned Session Topics

Each session covers one topic in depth, with real examples from the Stanford AIMI project where applicable. Sessions build on each other but are designed to be independently accessible.

  • 01

    Introduction to Machine Learning

    What ML actually is, how supervised learning works, and why it's different from traditional programming. The landscape: regression, classification, neural networks.

  • 02

    Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)

    How CNNs process images — convolution, pooling, feature maps. Why spatial structure matters and how CNNs learn to detect edges, textures, and objects.

  • 03

    Transfer Learning

    Why training from scratch is rarely the right answer. ImageNet pretraining, feature extraction vs. fine-tuning, and when each approach is appropriate.

  • 04

    DenseNet & EfficientNet Deep Dive

    The architectures behind our Stanford project. Dense blocks, compound scaling, CBAM attention — what the papers say and what actually matters in practice.

  • 05

    Medical Imaging & the CheXpert Dataset

    How chest X-ray classification works, why it's hard (multi-label, noisy labels, class imbalance), and how we handled these challenges in the Stanford project.

  • 06

    Evaluation Metrics — Beyond Accuracy

    Why accuracy is often meaningless in medicine. AUROC, sensitivity, specificity, F1, precision-recall curves, and how to interpret each in a clinical context.

  • 07

    Model Interpretability & Grad-CAM

    The black box problem in clinical AI. Gradient-weighted class activation mapping: how it works, how to generate heatmaps, and why it matters for trust in medical AI.

  • 08

    AI Ethics in Medicine

    Dataset bias, health equity, FDA regulation, and the question of when AI should (and shouldn't) augment clinical decision-making. A discussion-heavy session.

  • 09

    Student Project Showcase

    Members present their own AI explorations — Kaggle competitions, personal projects, research papers they've read. Open format, peer feedback.

  • 10

    The Future of AI in Medicine

    Pathology AI, robotic surgery, drug discovery, genomics. Where the field is heading and what role students today can play in shaping it.

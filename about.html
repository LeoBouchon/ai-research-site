Background

I'm a junior at Monta Vista High School in Cupertino, California, maintaining a 4.0 GPA across AP and honors coursework including AP Computer Science A and Chemistry Honors. My core interest sits at the intersection of medicine and technology — specifically, how machine learning can augment clinical decision-making and improve patient outcomes.

My path into AI and medicine isn't purely academic. I completed a pre-medical program at UC Berkeley where I rotated through Surgery, Internal Medicine, Pediatrics, Neurology, and Psychiatry, completed hands-on simulation training at UCSF's Kanbar Center, and earned CPR/AED certification. That clinical exposure is what motivates my AI work: I've seen firsthand how diagnostic bottlenecks affect patient care, and I want to help fix them.

Stanford AIMI Internship

From August 2025 to June 2026, I completed a year-long research internship at the Stanford Center for Artificial Intelligence in Medicine and Imaging (AIMI), mentored by Cally Lin and Dr. Curtis Langlotz. The program brought together high school students from across the country to develop machine learning models for early disease detection.

My focus was building a DenseNet121-based multi-label classifier to detect 14 thoracic pathologies from chest X-rays using the CheXpert Plus dataset (223,462 radiographs). Our team's model achieved a mean validation AUROC of 0.9011 — a 13.3% improvement over the DenseNet baseline.

This was a collaborative project. I led the DenseNet121 track, while teammates developed an EfficientNetB0 model with CBAM attention heads. Together we co-authored a research poster presented at the Stanford AIMI Center.

Learning Hub

Resources

Curated reading, courses, tools, and datasets for learning AI and medical imaging — organized by topic and skill level.

These are the resources I've actually used — not a generic list. Each one either helped me build something or changed how I think about a problem. I'll keep adding to this as the AI Club sessions develop.

Getting Started with ML

🎓

Supervised Machine Learning — Andrew Ng (Coursera)

The best introduction to ML I've found. Covers regression, classification, gradient descent, and regularization with clear math and strong intuitions. The course I completed through Stanford Online.

coursera.org ↗
📘

fast.ai — Practical Deep Learning for Coders

A top-down approach that gets you training real models immediately before diving into theory. Excellent for building intuition fast.

course.fast.ai ↗
▶️

3Blue1Brown — Neural Networks (YouTube)

The clearest visual explanation of how neural networks and backpropagation work. Watch this before anything else if you're new to the concept.

YouTube ↗

Convolutional Networks & Computer Vision

📄

Densely Connected Convolutional Networks (DenseNet Paper)

Huang et al., 2017. The original DenseNet paper — readable and well-structured. Essential reading for understanding why dense connectivity helps.

arxiv.org ↗
📄

CheXNet: Radiologist-Level Pneumonia Detection (Stanford)

Rajpurkar et al., 2017. The paper that showed DenseNet121 could match radiologist performance on chest X-ray classification. The direct precursor to the work I did at AIMI.

arxiv.org ↗
📘

CS231n: Convolutional Neural Networks for Visual Recognition (Stanford)

Stanford's definitive course on computer vision. Lecture notes are freely available and go deep on CNNs, architectures, and training.

cs231n.stanford.edu ↗

AI in Medicine

🏥

Stanford AIMI Center

The center where I completed my internship. Excellent source of papers, datasets, and open-source tools for medical AI research.

aimi.stanford.edu ↗
📊

CheXpert Plus Dataset

The dataset used in my internship project. 223,462 chest radiographs with 14 pathology labels. Freely available for research.

Redivis ↗
📄

Grad-CAM: Visual Explanations from Deep Networks

Selvaraju et al., 2017. The paper behind gradient-weighted class activation mapping — a key technique for making medical AI interpretable.

arxiv.org ↗

Tools & Environments

💻

Google Colab

Free GPU-backed Jupyter notebooks in the browser. The best way to start training models without needing your own hardware.

colab.research.google.com ↗
🏆

Kaggle

Competitions, free datasets, and community notebooks. A great place to practice on real problems and see how others approach the same challenges.

kaggle.com ↗
🤗

Hugging Face

Pretrained models, datasets, and the Transformers library. Increasingly relevant for vision-language models in medical AI.

huggingface.co ↗
🔥

PyTorch Documentation

The deep learning framework used in the AIMI internship. Clear documentation and a strong community. Start with the official 60-minute blitz.

pytorch.org/tutorials ↗

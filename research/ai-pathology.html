Project Overview

Building directly on the DenseNet121 model developed during the Stanford AIMI internship, this project aims to improve upon that foundation in two key dimensions: model performance (pushing AUROC higher through architectural and training refinements) and pathology coverage (expanding detection beyond the initial 5-class subset).

The internship model achieved a strong mean AUROC of 0.9011, but known limitations — difficulty distinguishing visually similar pathologies, class imbalance sensitivity, and the absence of attention visualization — represent clear engineering and research opportunities.

Research Goals

  • Improve mean AUROC beyond 0.9011 through architectural and training changes
  • Expand the pathology set — targeting the full 14-label CheXpert taxonomy or a broader subset
  • Implement Grad-CAM visualization to produce interpretable, clinically meaningful attention maps
  • Address class imbalance more robustly (weighted loss, oversampling, or focal loss)
  • Explore ensemble approaches combining DenseNet and attention-based architectures
  • Validate generalization across external datasets beyond CheXpert

Dataset

This project will continue to use CheXpert Plus (223,462 radiographs, 64,725 patients) as the primary training corpus, targeting the full 14-pathology label set. The same U-Ones preprocessing policy will be applied, with additional investigation into alternative uncertainty handling strategies (U-Zeros, U-Ignore) to determine which yields the best balance of sensitivity and specificity per class.

External validation on NIH ChestX-ray14 is planned as a generalization test — a critical step for any model intended for clinical screening use.
